What is Civil Engineering?

Civil engineering, the profession of designing and executing structural works that serve the general public, such as dams, bridges,
aqueducts, canals, highways, power plants, sewerage systems, and other infrastructure.From earliest times, however, engineers have engaged in peaceful activities, and many of the civil engineering works of ancient and medieval times—such as the Roman public baths, roads, bridges, and aqueducts; the Flemish canals; the Dutch sea defenses; the French Gothic cathedrals; and many other monuments—reveal a history of inventive genius and persistent experimentation.

Some Career options in Civil Engineering

Structural engineer

Structural engineers are the ones who design and analyze the structures. Therefore, they get the correct information about the safety and stability of the structures. This career path demands strong mathematics and physics knowledge, as well as excellent problem-solving skills.

Environmental engineer

As society becomes more environmentally aware, the responsibilities of an environmental engineer have gained more value. Environmental engineers work on projects that focus on enhancing environmental quality, like air pollution control, waste management, water, and sustainable growth

Transportation engineering

Transportation is an important part of modern society, and transportation engineers play an important role in designing and maintaining the best transport systems.Transportation engineers need outstanding analytical knowledge and a good understanding of traffic flow, safety regulations, and transportation technology.

Urban planner

Urban planning is a great career choice for those interested in shaping the growth of cities and communities. Urban planners work on building effective functional and attractive, pleasing urban spaces. They collaborate with government organizations and the public to design and implement urban plans.

Geotechnical Engineer

Geotechnical engineering is the best choice. Geotechnical engineers use soil and rock mechanics as the basis of their design for structures such as foundations, retaining walls, and others. Apart from that, they ensure that all parts are stable under any type of situation, including difficult areas.

Construction Engineer

Construction engineers are crucial in planning and designing the construction projects. They closely collaborate with designers, contractors, and other participants to guarantee that the projects are implemented successfully.

Construction Manager

Construction managers are professionals who oversee and direct construction activities in a building or structure project. They are responsible for creating cost estimates, work timetables, and devising budgets. Construction managers also comply with state and federal legal requirements including business and safety codes. It is one of the finest careers after civil engineering.

Fire Engineer

> Site Engineer

Site Engineers oversee construction progress, ensuring that the project follows the design and stay on schedule.While the role is traditionally hands-on with little to no technology involvement, incorporation of BIM workflows has opened up a whole new array of jobs on site.

Consulting Engineer

Consulting engineers work to oversee and formulate plans and strategies for improving the performance of buildings or structures. These professionals are specialized in project design, coordination, and analysis. Consulting engineers provide insights for innovative and effective design strategies for private or public infrastructure projects. Consulting engineer is a great choice for a civil engineering career.

Nuclear Engineer

Nuclear engineers are professionals who are responsible for researching new uses and management of nuclear power or materials. They design and develop nuclear equipment like radiation shielding and reactor cores. Nuclear engineers are responsible for examining nuclear accidents and analyzing data for designing prevention strategies. This is a highly demanding job after civil engineering.

Surveyor

Surveyors measure and note property boundaries and locations to determine building site specifications. The surveyor researches records or land titles to verify the accuracy of the data and present their findings to designated authorities. Surveyors prepare maps or plots to establish water or land boundaries in regards to deeds, leases and other legal documents.
